# Fold up, scissor lift, 3D Printer

<img width="2560" height="1440" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1136d976-1181-4ed2-ad26-718f32674507" />

This is my fold up, scissor lift, cross-cantilever 3D printer for Open Sauce 2026! It folds up into the size of a small filament box, coming in at just 200x200x75mm in form factor, and has a build area of nearly 80x80x95mm! It is a fully unique 3D printer that utilizes a Positron hotend and a custom HGX Lite bowden extruder and all of it runs off of a simple barrel-jack connector. 

## Custom Features
- Unique scissor-lift, cross-cantilever motion system
- Fits in a filament box and folds up into a 200x200x75mm form factor
- Has a build area of almost 80x80x95mm
- Runs completely off a portable barrel jack connector
- Offline printing using a MicroSD card
- Fast and efficient positron hotend with a custom HGX Lite bowden extruder
- Supports Marlin or Klipper firmware with the BTT SKR MINI E3 V3.0 motherboard
- Looks insanely cool so you can flex at hackathons

## Assembly

### Bill of Materials (BOM)

I bought my parts from Aliexpress, Amazon and then got the aluminum plates cut by PCBWay. 

The BOM isn't too complicated and it also includes some tools that are helpful for assembly, you can access [it here](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1xFZ-rjcnBZJ-4SHDmdkzTACUUDvWx6YCcqEs-uq4CdA/edit?usp=sharing).

### CAD

Assembling the board is fairly simple by referencing the [Onshape assembly](https://cad.onshape.com/documents/3a7b565130dc54a0f58628c0/w/db45ce09661f59e4e89ec29b/e/58f0074d6e4db55998381609?configuration=default&renderMode=0&uiState=6a406a14ea7b36fb18681a02).

All the 3D printed/aluminum parts and the entire STEP for the project are accessibly in the [CAD folder](/CAD) too. 

### Firmware

You can just run Marlin firmware for the entire printer. I've left some boilerplate in the [firmware folder](/firmware)!
